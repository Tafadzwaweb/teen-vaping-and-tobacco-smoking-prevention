Needs Assessment

Why this is a crisis
we can't ignore.

Vaping didn't emerge by accident — it was designed to be appealing, discreet, and addictive. Here's what's driving the epidemic among teens today.

Flavors as a Gateway

E-cigarettes come in thousands of youth-appealing flavors — mango, cotton candy, mint — specifically engineered to make nicotine feel harmless and fun. Flavored products account for the vast majority of teen vaping initiation.

Designed to Be Hidden

Many vapes look like USB drives or pens. Students use them in classrooms, bathrooms, and hallways undetected. The discreet design was not an accident — it lowers the perceived risk for teens.

Nicotine Misconceptions

A significant majority of teen vapers do not realize their device contains nicotine at all. One popular pod device delivers as much nicotine as an entire pack of cigarettes — without any of the visible warning signs of traditional smoking.

Peer Modeling & Social Media

Teens dramatically overestimate how common vaping is among their peers. Social media makes vaping appear mainstream and cool. In reality, the majority of teens do not vape — but the perception drives behavior.

Health Facts

Myths vs. Reality.

The vaping industry profits from misinformation. Here's what the science actually says — no hype, just facts.

🚫 The Myth
✅ The Reality
"It's just water vapor — totally harmless."
Vape aerosol contains nicotine, heavy metals (lead, nickel, tin), diacetyl (linked to lung disease), and ultrafine particles that penetrate deep into lung tissue.
"Everyone at school vapes."
Only about 10% of teens nationwide currently use e-cigarettes. It feels universal because vaping is visible and talked about — the non-users are simply quiet.
"I can quit whenever I want."
Nicotine is among the most addictive substances known. Most teen vapers who try to quit report significant withdrawal symptoms and repeated failed attempts.
"Flavored vapes are safer than cigarettes."
Flavoring chemicals like diacetyl add additional toxic risk. "Safer than cigarettes" does not mean safe — it means two harmful things being compared to each other.

Health Behavior Theory

Why teens start —
and how to stop it.

This campaign is grounded in Social Cognitive Theory (Albert Bandura), which explains that behavior is shaped by what we observe, what we believe we can do, and what outcomes we expect. Here's how each construct applies.

01
SCT Construct

Self-Efficacy

Your belief in your own power to say no. When self-efficacy is high, peer pressure loses its grip. This campaign teaches refusal skills to actively build it — because efficacy is a skill, not a personality trait.

02
SCT Construct

Outcome Expectations

Teens often overestimate the social benefits of vaping and underestimate the health risks. By replacing inaccurate expectations with evidence-based facts, this campaign shifts the cost-benefit calculation teens make unconsciously.

03
SCT Construct

Observational Learning

We learn by watching others. The same peer modeling that drives initiation can drive prevention. By elevating teens who choose not to vape as respected role models, we reshape the social norm — from the inside out.

Take Action

Your power
to say no.

Self-efficacy isn't about willpower. It's a learnable skill. Here are four research-backed strategies to navigate pressure in the moment.

01

The Direct Refusal

Simple, firm, and no explanation needed. You don't owe anyone a reason for your choices. Practice saying it until it feels natural.

"No thanks, I'm good."
02

Change the Subject

Redirect the moment before it gets awkward. You take control of the situation by shifting to something else entirely.

"Nah, let's go grab something to eat."
03

Invoke an External Reason

Take the social pressure off yourself personally by citing an outside constraint. Sports, family, or activities all work.

"Coach tests us — I can't risk it."
04

Find Your People

Research shows that having even one close friend who doesn't use substances significantly reduces your own risk of initiation. Community is protection.

Your crew shapes your choices — choose them wisely.
